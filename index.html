History of JDM Legends

From humble beginnings to global icons

1960s - The Dawn

Japan's automotive industry rises, with models like the Toyota 2000GT showcasing engineering excellence.

1980s - Performance Era

The Toyota AE86 became a symbol of lightweight, rear-wheel-drive performance. Its role in motorsports and pop culture — especially drifting and Initial D — helped define JDM car culture for a generation.

1990s - Golden Age

Icons like the Supra MK4, RX-7 FD, and GT-R R34 dominate both street and track scenes.

2000s - Tuning Culture

Street racing and drift culture explode, with JDM cars becoming symbols of customization and individuality.

2010s-2020s - Global Revival

Classic JDM cars gain collector status, while modern models like the new Supra and GT-R continue the legacy.
